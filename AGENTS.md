# Public Website Copy Rules

This site is a public-facing PaintersGO portfolio/product website. Visible UI copy must speak to visitors, not to the developer implementing the page.

- Do not publish internal planning language such as "organized by readiness", "moved from another page", "this belongs here", "keep this content", "in active refinement", or "production-facing workflow".
- Replace implementation notes with visitor-facing value: what the viewer can understand, try, read, or trust.
- Labels under cards should introduce the content itself, not explain why the content exists in the codebase.
- When showing project archive material, describe it as a story, note, case study, or reading. Do not mention previous placement, migration, grouping decisions, or editorial cleanup.
- For unfinished or experimental features, say what direction PaintersGO is exploring. Do not expose task status, backlog status, or acceptance criteria.
- Before finishing a content change, scan visible strings for words that sound like requirements or code comments, and rewrite them into natural product/portfolio copy.
